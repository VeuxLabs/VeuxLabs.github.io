  • Lightspeed FlightLink System

    Project

    FlightLink Connected System
    iOS Universal App

    Client

    Lightspeed Navigation

    Challenge

    IN-FLIGHT COMMUNICATIONS CHALLENGES
    Aircraft cockpits are complicated, placing a high cognitive load on pilots. With so many things to monitor, unclear or missed instructions from air traffic control add stress and risk to the flying experience. Asking the tower to repeat instructions takes additional time, can slow take-offs or landings, and can be embarrassing for some pilots. The FlightLink system offers a solution to these challenges.

    Main View with Writing Pad

    Solution

    FLYING WITH APP SUPPORT
    Our researchers interviewed and observed private pilots to learn about their challenges and their use of mobile devices during flight. iPads are already commonly used for pre-flight checks and in-flight navigation. This provided a platform for an innovative, simple to use flight voice recording app. A unique hardware feature allows all cockpit intercom and radio traffic to be mixed and then output to the iPad's auxiliary port or Bluetooth connection, where it is then recorded by the FlightLink app.

    Headset Connection and Setup

    TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION
    All aircraft audio from the headset is recorded using either an analog output or a digital Bluetooth connection, which uses the Apple iPod Accessory Protocol and the External Accessory Framework. The app has been implemented as a universal app for iPad, iPhone & iPod. The system is certified under Appleâ€™s Made For iPod (MFi) program.

    DESIGN SOLUTION
    Given the busy and complex environment of the cockpit, the app had to be simple and easy to use. An innovative user interface allows simple one-touch access to any part of the last two minutes of recorded audio. A digital notepad gives the pilot an easy way to jot down instructions or other relevant information.
