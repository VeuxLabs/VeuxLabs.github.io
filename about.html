Karl Allen

PARTNER, DIRECTOR OF PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT

Karl knows software, Karl knows hardware, and most of all, Karl knows execution. As head of Engineering Services for Veux, Karl is responsible for our mobile software and systems architecture, Agile process and project management. Read more.. Before co-founding Veux, Karl was with NetApp and at LSI, where he was Director of Technology and Quality. Prior, he was CTO at Planet Halo and was responsible for developing smartphones, online services, and data gateways. Before Halo, Karl spent a decade with Intel, starting as a Software Architect in Intel’s Architecture Labs. While with Intel’s Labs, he led a team of software and hardware engineers in the design and development of the world’s first wireless internet tablet for consumers. Karl was also with Intel’s New Business Group and was a senior member of the team that created Intel’s Home Medical Monitoring Business. He was also a founder and leader of Intel’s Point of Care / Internet Health Initiative where he led the definition and development of web-based health services and a speech-enabled wireless handheld device for health professionals.

Steve Brown

FOUNDER, PARTNER, DIRECTOR OF USER EXPERIENCE

As Head of User Experience at Veux, Steve sees UX as an engine of business value, value generated by designing products that people need and desire. It’s a view matured over two decades experience leading teams in the conceptualization and design of mobile and consumer technology products. Read more.. Prior to Veux, Steve was Senior Director of the Design and User Experience Group at Avaya, where he was responsible for the unified communications, collaboration and contact center portfolios. While there, he led the design of application user experiences for smartphones, tablets, notebooks, desktops and embedded systems. Before Avaya he led the corporate “Center-of-Excellence” for design and user experience at Nortel. Prior to Nortel, Steve spent eight years as a partner at Fiori, an award winning product design firm in Portland. Steve was also Director of Product Design at Ziba, and a product development manager at Intel, where he received the Intel Achievement Award.
